The Numbers Behind the Paradox

Numbers aren't just numbers—they represent lives. In the Philippines, the coexistence of malnutrition and obesity reveals deep-rooted inequities in access to nutritious food. These statistics—from stunting in children to obesity in adults—illustrate how food insecurity manifests in dual health burdens.

National Overview (2023 FNRI & DOH Data)

  • Adult Obesity: Approximately 4 in every 10 Filipino adults (39.8%) are classified as obese, with prevalence significantly higher among women (45.4%) and urban residents (44.5%).
  • Household Food Insecurity: Roughly 31.4% of households experience moderate to severe food insecurity, while 2.7% face severe food insecurity.
  • Child Stunting: Around 23.6% of children under 5 years are stunted, and 15.1% are underweight.

Age Group Breakdown

  • Children (5–10 years): About 14% are overweight or obese.
  • Adolescents (10–19 years): Approximately 13% are overweight or obese.
  • Adults (20+ years): Overweight or obesity rates hit 40.2% in total adult population.

Regional & Urban-Rural Disparities

Metro Manila (Adults 20–59 years):

  • Overweight: 28.8%
  • Obese: 9.6%

Among elderly Filipinos (60+): 24.7% overweight, 6.3% obese. Cities in NCR report even higher rates—up to 14% obesity among seniors.

Regional Hotspots in Child Stunting:

BARMM (Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao) exhibits the highest stunting prevalence at 34.3%, one of the highest in the country.

Historical & Global Context

Over time, one in three Filipino children under age five was stunted—29% in 2019, with only slight improvement since 2008 (from 32%).

The World Bank ranks the Philippines among the top 10 countries globally for stunting prevalence—underscoring its persistent nutritional challenge.

The Overlap: Hidden Hunger

Many Filipinos face a paradox where they are overweight yet nutritionally deficient—so-called “hidden hunger.” Diets heavy in processed and calorie-dense food often lack essential vitamins and minerals, particularly among low-income populations.
